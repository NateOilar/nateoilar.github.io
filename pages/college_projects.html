Liminal Forest

This is a 3D liminal scary forest environment that I created in unreal engine 5 over the course of 14 weeks. This far and remote place is sat atop a very large mountain with the only way in or out being the pich black tunnels leading into the side of the moutain. The goal is to wander and search through the thick fog for the missing three capitan america plushies hidden at key POIs. There are abandoned cabins, a rusted and creeky playground, watchtowers, and a half submerged jeep in the nearby lake that you should make sure to look for. All of this built and seemingly recently accupied by people, but for some reason... no one is to be seen, at all... Happy hunting!

This was my first ever time creating anything in UE5 ever and it was really fun to experimnet and tinker around with procedurally generated content, material creation, skybox lighting and fog, and 3D modeling from scratch within UE5. With the grass and trees within the environment, are (PG) content that we learned to implement in class. There were issues at first with placing the assets and it generating to many of them and crashing the engine. making smaller areas to be affected helped with this issue and allowed the enironment to run in a more stable fashion. I created custom materials for this project as well and added them to the playground within the environment. With reguards to the playground, that is an asset I created entirly from scratch for this environment. The lighting and fog elements posted their own set of challenges and reserch to understand fully, I feel as tho I have the bacis understood and implimented well in this environment. Finally I added alot of ambiant sounds to really make the environment feel scary and alive. The plushies have attenuation attatched and a sound that lets you know your close. The same technique was used for the pond ambient sounds.

Vyre's Veil

The team and I's interactive comic is for education on the bi-directional flow of time in relation to quantum physics for individuals at the 5th grade level and up. There is a short 45 sec animation of our main character “Vyer” who is falling into a black hole as the intro into the comic. As he falls, his important life memories start to fly by him. This fades out and is followed by text to help the player think about what just happened. Then the player is given the overall view of panels 1-5 where they can click on them in order, to help set up “Vyre” to meet himself but his 3 1/2 dimensional self. On panel 5 the player clicks on “Vyre's” other self, a short animation plays to show that the two are becoming one and the player is entering the mind. The player then gets an overview of panels 6-11 which are randomized and can be viewed in any order. All these panels are memories that the player must decipher for a puzzle later in the game. Once all the panels have been viewed the player moves onto the next scene. This scene holds panels 12-16 which can also be viewed in any order and are randomized just like 6-11 and will be used for a puzzle later in the game.

I was one of the artist and the sound designer for this project which was a more important role than I first thought. As my role for the artist, I did the planning, sketching, prototyping, and final versions of comic book panels 12, 17-22. Panels 18-22 are mirror reflections of panels 1-5. This is to help the player realize that our main character has helped him remember how he arrived in the 3 1/2 dimension. This then allows the player to escape the black hole he fell into and returns to the 3rd dimension. I also was the individual that had the privlage of creating a great sound scape to compliment playing our game. The script that goes along with the music allows each background music file to be attached to the scene and when the player clicks the respective button to change scenes the current music playing on that scene fades out and the new music fades in along with the scene. This allows for all the sounds and music that plays within the interactive comic to immerse the player further within the story. After Panel 22 there is a puzzle where all 10 of the panels 6-16 are mixed up and the player must put them in order. This signifies that Vyre can now remember how he ended up in the 3.5th dimension. Once the player completes the puzzle there are two animations that play. The first of the two animations shows Vyre with his new eyes and no mouth falling back up through the black hole with those same memories flying in random past him. Then the second of the two animations plays which show all the memories falling past the screen slowly and in reverse order to show him remembering back on his life and this has freed him from the 3.5th dimensional prison he had been stuck in.
